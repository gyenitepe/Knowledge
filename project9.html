Fixed Bid Target Beta Strategy

The ultimate purpose of the Beta program is to prove that Fixed Bid Target is a product that can make meaningful impact to our business in practice and is worth the investments to build out a full-fledged GA product.

Program Overview

The Fixed Bid Target Beta program is strategically designed to validate the product's impact and inform future investment. Our existing customer base is segmented into three key categories, each with distinct needs and behaviors that inform our Beta focus.

Customer Categories

  • 1. Scale or Good Performance: Customers aiming to grow or maintain high efficiency.
  • 2. New Customers: Those new to our platform or fixed budget pacing.
  • 3. Everyone Else: The broader customer base, including those with varying needs.

Beta Focus Rationale

The Beta program primarily focuses on categories #1 and #2 due to their strong product-market fit and openness to new business models.

  • Category #1: Proven strongest product-market fit for Fixed Bid Target.
  • Category #2: No prior experience with existing fixed budget pacing, making them more receptive.

While category #3 is not an immediate Beta focus, we will be actively gathering data to identify enablers, such as confidence intervals for spend, crucial for gaining buy-in, as this segment accounts for the largest portion of our Gross Revenue (GR).

Illustration of the Beta program's strategic focus areas.

Objectives: Customers Who Want to Scale or Have Good Performance

For customers seeking to scale their operations or optimize performance, the Beta program aims to validate our capabilities in onboarding large-scale campaigns, achieving desired outcomes, and ensuring long-term product stickiness.

Objectives: New Customers

Engaging new customers involves validating their acceptance of our new launch methodology and ensuring operational efficiency despite initial campaign volatility.

Current historical success rate for new launch campaigns, highlighting the challenge FBT aims to improve.

Open Question: Isolating FBT Impact on New Launches

New launches are currently susceptible to spend spikes or poor performance, with 75% failing. Fixed Bid Targets may improve performance but not entirely resolve these inherent problems.

Initial thought: If a customer churns due to spend volatility, the Fixed Bid Target product is responsible. If churn is due to performance (expected to be better with FBT), we do not believe it is solely due to this product. This distinction is crucial for accurate evaluation.

Objectives: Everyone Else

For the majority of our customer base, the primary objective is to develop a seamless transition plan, with a focus on establishing confidence intervals for spend to garner crucial stakeholder buy-in.

Overall Program Objective

Beyond category-specific goals, a key overarching objective for the Beta program is to integrate Fixed Bid Target into our broader budget and margin management strategies.
